# Manager Guide — Visory Performance & Growth

Your job each quarter isn't to grade people — it's to **see them clearly and do one useful thing about it.** This guide takes you through the whole loop: set up, assess, run the 1:1, and close out — plus the board that keeps it all on time. None of it is heavy, and the app does the structure-keeping for you. Let's make this the rhythm your team relies on.

---

## Your nav

Alongside your own items (Dashboard, My Reviews, Goals & Metrics, Tasks), you have a **Team** section:

| Open this | When you want to |
|---|---|
| **My Team** | See your reports, their cycle status, and where to start |
| **Assessment Cycles** | Track every report through the cycle, against deadlines |
| **Analysis** | Calibrate — see distribution, trends, and prescribed actions across people |

## First, set the ground truth

A cycle without metrics and goals is just impressions. **Before your team's first self-assessment week, set each report up for success.** Open their profile from **My Team → View Profile** and load:

- **Key Metrics** (**Add Metric**) — recurring, measurable, owned by them. *Monthly revenue retained ≥ 95%*, *Response time < 4 business hours*. **Aim for 3–5.** Skip vague ones like "be a good team player" — that belongs in the values conversation.
- **Goals** (**Add Goal**) — time-bound, specific, with a finish line. *Migrate the top 10 clients by 30 June.* **Aim for 2–4 live.** Goals should mean stretch or change, not business-as-usual.

Both of you can see these any time, each tagged with who set it — so there are no surprises. **If you're unsure what to load for someone, decide it together in your next 1:1.** That conversation *is* the work, and it's a strong one to have.

## Know the rhythm — and the deadlines built in for you

The app tracks each report against three milestones (anchored to the final month of the quarter). **Watch them on the Assessment Cycles board** — each card flags itself **on track**, **due soon**, or **overdue**, so you never have to hold the calendar in your head:

| Milestone | By |
|---|---|
| **Ready to Meet** — both assessments submitted | 10th |
| **Meeting Complete** — 1:1 held | 20th |
| **Results Sent** — shared back | 25th |

Cards flow left to right: **In Assessment → Ready to Meet → Meeting Scheduled → Meeting Complete → Review Complete.** Once both assessments are in, a card moves to *Ready to Meet* on its own — **you drag it forward from there.**

## Assess your team (Week 1)

From **My Team**, click **Assess** on a report. It's a 5-step form, and **every rating step has a Comments field — use it to capture the example behind the number.** Your report self-assesses in parallel, and **you won't see their answers until both are submitted.** That independence protects the honesty of the whole process, so trust it.

Work the five steps:

1. **Performance** — Below / Meeting / Exceeding, against role requirements. *(First, record an actual result for each Key Metric — the form asks for this so your rating sits on data, not memory.)*
2. **Growth Readiness** — **Developing / Building / Ready Now.** How ready are they for more scope, and on what horizon? Read it as observable readiness, never ambition or personality.
3. **Values** — rate each of the four (Customer First, Step into the Arena, Flock to Problems, Give Energy), then add examples.
4. **Engagement** — energy, discretionary effort, contribution.
5. **Additional notes** — anything else worth bringing to the conversation.

**Hold to three principles — they keep you fair:**

- **Be specific.** Every rating deserves at least one concrete example you're ready to share.
- **Rate the work, not the person.**
- **Don't anchor to their self-assessment.** The gap between your views is the gold — don't pre-soften a number to avoid a hard moment. You can hold a high standard *and* be kind; this framework lets you do both.

## What your ratings produce

Open **Review Summary** once both assessments are in. The app places each person on two grids, each with a ready-made action:

**Talent Density — Performance × Growth Readiness** (the capability lens)

| Growth ↓ / Perf → | Below | Meeting | Exceeding |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ready Now** | Remove the Blockers | Stretch & Develop | **Ready to Promote** |
| **Building** | Build the Foundations | Keep Growing | Expand the Role |
| **Developing** | Intervene Now | Maintain & Recognise | Deepen the Expertise |

**Cultural Momentum — Values × Engagement** (the culture & motivation lens)

| Engagement ↓ / Values → | Below | Meeting | Exceeding |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Exceeding** | Redirect the Energy | Reinforce the Behaviours | **Protect & Amplify** |
| **Meeting** | Have the Conversation | Stay the Course | Invest Deeper |
| **Below** | Assess the Fit | Find What's Draining | Re-energise Now |

Each cell carries a concrete next step — *Ready to Promote* → "Career conversation. Discuss promotion or expanded scope this quarter." The Summary also shows a side-by-side **Assessment Comparison** of self vs. manager. **Read it before you walk in** — that's how you spot the gap worth talking about.

## Run the 1:1 (Week 2)

45 minutes, structured, everything shared openly. **Hit Start Meeting** to open the notes page, and follow the shape:

- **0–5 — Check-in.** Start human. How are they, really?
- **5–15 — Their self-assessment.** Listen. Ask, don't correct.
- **15–25 — Your Performance & Growth Readiness feedback,** with rationale. Compare with their view.
- **25–30 — Your Values & Engagement feedback,** value by value.
- **30–40 — The gap.** The richest part. What aren't they seeing? What aren't you? Stay curious.
- **40–45 — Actions.** Agree next steps and any goal updates.

**Lead with the rating, then explain it.** **Ask "Does this feel fair?" — and mean it.** If they bring evidence, be willing to move. Separate the work from the person: *"Your output on the BAS lodgements was below what we need"* is feedback they can act on; *"you're not good enough"* is not.

In the meeting page, **capture Actions — each becomes a tracked Task** with an owner and due date, visible to both of you. Close every loop in writing. No verbal agreements that quietly disappear.

---

## Close it out

On the **Summary** page, click **Send Results to [name].** This opens their My Reviews — ratings, rationale, actions, and trend. **Send only after the 1:1 has happened** (the app will remind you). Made a mistake? **Unlock & edit** reopens the assessment — every change is audit-logged — and you can re-send. You're never locked into an error.

---

## The tools beyond the cycle

- **Tasks** — set each to **Shared** or **Manager only**, and filter your team's tasks by person or source (Manual / Meeting action / Performance plan).
- **Performance Improvement Plan** — for a struggling report, start a manager-only **PIP** from their profile, then run it through structured check-in meetings and actions. Use it to give someone a fair, clear path back.
- **Review Notes** — attach notes, meeting links, transcripts, or summaries to a cycle so the context lives with the record.
- **Analysis** — calibrate. See how your team spreads across both grids, who's top talent, who's at risk, and how people moved quarter on quarter. **Bring your high-potential and high-concern people to your review with People** — you don't carry these calls alone.

---

## Your quarterly five — for every report

After each cycle, answer five quick questions per person. They turn a rating into a decision:

1. What's their **Talent Density** action — and the one thing *you'll* do?
2. What's their **Cultural Momentum** action — and the one thing *you'll* do?
3. Do the two conflict? Which wins?
4. What's the trend — improving, stable, or declining?
5. What will *you* do differently this quarter?

Do this well and your team will feel it: seen clearly, backed genuinely, and growing on purpose. That's the whole point.
