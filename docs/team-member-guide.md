# Team Member Guide — Visory Performance & Growth

Welcome. This is your space to reflect, be seen clearly, and grow on purpose — one quarter at a time. It takes about 30 focused minutes a quarter, and this guide walks you through every step. You've got this.

> **The point isn't your score. It's the gap between how you see your quarter and how your manager sees it.** That gap is where the best conversations happen — so lean into it.

---

## How the quarter works

Each quarter runs one simple loop: **you reflect → your manager assesses → you meet → you act.**

You and your manager complete your assessments *independently* — neither of you sees the other's until your 1:1. That's deliberate, and it's on your side: it means you both arrive honest and uninfluenced. Everything is then shared openly. Nothing is hidden from you.

You'll live mostly in four places on the left-hand menu:

| Open this | When you want to |
|---|---|
| **Dashboard** | See where you are in the cycle and what to do next |
| **My Reviews** | Read your results and trace your progress over time |
| **Goals & Metrics** | Check — and set — what you're measured against |
| **Tasks** | Work the actions you agreed with your manager |

---

## First, make your Goals & Metrics yours

**Open Goals & Metrics now.** You'll see two lists, each tagged **Set by manager** or **Set by employee**:

- **Key Metrics** — the recurring numbers that show what "good" looks like in your role.
- **Goals** — time-bound commitments with a clear finish line.

**Don't wait to be told what matters — add your own.** Use **Add Goal** and **Add Metric** to put forward what *you* believe you should be measured on. It's one of the best ways to open a 1:1, and it shows ownership. Keep it focused: a handful of metrics and two to four live goals. A few sharp commitments will always beat a long, fuzzy list — and you'll feel the difference.

---

## Week 1 — Your self-assessment

When the cycle opens, your **Dashboard** shows a **Start Self-Assessment** button. **Click it.** It's an 8-step form, and there's no pressure to finish in one sitting — **hit Save Draft anytime and come back.** Nothing is final until you press **Submit** on the last step.

Move through the eight steps:

1. **Performance** — rate yourself (Below / Meeting / Exceeding) and note the evidence behind it.
2. **Key achievements** — name 2–3 things you're genuinely proud of.
3. **Blockers** — be honest about what slowed you down.
4. **Learning** — what you picked up, and the one skill that would lift you next quarter.
5. **Values** — rate each of the four, then give real examples:
   - Think Customer First · Step into the Arena · Flock to Problems · Give Energy
6. **Engagement** — how connected and motivated you felt, and what drove it.
7. **Support needed** — tell your manager what would genuinely help.
8. **Goals for next quarter** — these save straight into your tracked goals.

On the Performance step you'll see your live Goals & Metrics, so anchor your rating in the real numbers.

**Three things that make yours land — try them:**

- **Be specific.** "I turned around BAS lodgements for 12 clients two days early" tells the whole story. "I did well" tells none of it.
- **Be honest, not polished.** If it was a hard quarter, say so. Self-awareness is a strength here, and it's safe to show it — rating yourself below where your manager lands is completely okay.
- **Finish before your 1:1.** Give yourself the gift of walking in prepared.

---

## Week 2 — Your 1:1

This is a 45-minute conversation, and it's the most valuable meeting of your quarter. Your manager opens with a check-in, then you walk them through your self-assessment. After that, they share *all* of their feedback — Performance, Growth Readiness, each value, and Engagement — with the reasoning behind every rating.

Then you compare notes. **Your manager will ask "Does this feel fair?" — answer honestly.** If a rating doesn't match your evidence, say so and show your working. This is a two-way conversation, and your voice belongs in it.

**Know this going in:** your manager leads with the rating, *then* explains it. The first time, that can feel direct — that's normal, and it isn't personal. Every rating is about your work and behaviours, never your character. You'll leave with written action items, visible to both of you in **Tasks**.

---

## After the 1:1 — Your Results

Once your manager sends results, **My Reviews** opens up. Take a few minutes to read it properly:

- **Talent Density** (Performance × Growth Readiness) and **Cultural Momentum** (Values × Engagement) — where you sit on each, with a clear action attached.
- **Assessment Comparison** — click any dimension to see your view next to your manager's, side by side.
- **Manager Notes**, your **Learning** and **Goals for next quarter**, and a **History** of the cycle.
- A **Performance Summary** that builds a running average as the quarters add up.

> **Growth Readiness** — your readiness to take on more, and on what timeline — is your manager's call and appears here. Read it as honest timing, not a verdict on your ambition. If you want more scope, say so; this is exactly the place to start that.

If anything doesn't match what you discussed, raise it with your manager. You're meant to.

---

## Weeks 3–4 — Act

This is where the quarter pays off. **Work your Tasks** (To Do → In Progress → Done) and **keep your Goals moving** — mark each **On track**, **Off track**, or **Complete**, and drop a quick progress note. **Comment on any task** to keep the thread with your manager alive between cycles. You don't have to wait for the next 1:1 to ask for help.

---

## This record is yours

Every quarter adds to your **My Reviews** history — where you're consistently strong, where you tend to dip, and how your self-view tracks against your manager's over time. **Own it.** It's built to power your development, not just to deliver feedback once a quarter. Come back to it, look for the patterns, and use them to decide where to push next.

**Your quarterly checklist:**

- [ ] Goals & Metrics current — including ones you set
- [ ] Self-assessment submitted before the 1:1
- [ ] Showed up with specific evidence
- [ ] Said what you actually thought
- [ ] Tasks and goals updated through the month
